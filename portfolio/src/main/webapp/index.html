What makes me, me?




Hello. My name is Parth Vora. I am currently a sophomore studying at Rutgers University in New Jersey. I am a first generation student studying Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) and Computer Science (CS). I grew up in Jersey City, NJ which has been the number one most diverse city in the US for several years in a row. It is also the city that technically owns the Statue of Liberty. I attended McNair Academic High School - a public school with a private school dress code. Being exposed to this level of diversity and professionalism throughout my life, has severly impacted who I am and who I surround myself around.

Throughout high school, I was involved in several extracurriculars- including teaching students using Google CS First, volunteering at the Liberty Science Center, and working with our school's chapter of the National Honor Society to help out with community events. I am glad that I was able to give back to my community when I had the chance.

After high school, I was unsure about whether I wanted to pursue a career in computer science or business. Computer science - I was interested in but scared to pursue. Business - I hated, but it was a safe option. Luckily, I was accepted into Google CSSI, where I met 29 other very accomplished students with similar goals and ambitions. In a short 3 weeks, we became unexpectedly close. To this day, we hang out and even attend hackathons together.

In college, I have become an active leader in the Honors Academy in the School of Engineering. I help interview perspective students and run panels to answer FAQ's. I love to get involved, and I'm glad Google SPS will be a step in the right direction.
